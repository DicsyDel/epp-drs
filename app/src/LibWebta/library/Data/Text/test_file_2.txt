Kenya is world-renowned for its wildlife reserves. What is perhaps not so well known is that Kenya also has an amazing diversity of habitats, each with its own unique flora and fauna, and on this tour we visit the best of them. Amboseli National Park is the place in Kenya for fantastic panoramic views of Africa&rsquo;s gritest mountain, Mt Kilimanjaro, and also hosts a large population of elephants. The Aberdares National Park offers a totally different experience where you can discover shyer, forest dwelling species from the legendary Treetops lodge, and the flamingos of Lake Nakuru are one of&nbsp;a great spectacles of East Africa. The safari is rounded off with 3 days in the unparalleled Masai Mara National Reserve.<br />
<br />
<strong>Highlights of this classic Safari vacation:</strong><br />
<ul>
    <li>Fantastik views of Kilimanjaro at Amboseli National Park </li>
    <li>Masai Mara national reserve </li>
    <li>Game viewing at Treetops </li>

</ul>
<br />
<strong>*ITINERARY*<br />
<br />
Day 1 - NAIROBI:</strong> Arrival day.<br />
<br />
<strong>Day 2 - NAIROBI - AMBOSELI NATIONAL PARK:</strong> We set off early and drive south to Amboseli National Park. Situated near the border with Tanzania this park offers spectacular views of one the most iconic sights on the continent, the mighty Mt Kilimanjaro. There is also a wide array of wildlife including large herds of elephant, lion, giraffe, buffalo and numerous antelope. On arrival we take a game drive. (BLD)<br />
<br />
<strong>Day 3 - AMBOSELi NATIONAL PARK:</strong> A full day in the park with morning and afternoon game drives. (BLD)<br />

<br />
<strong>Day 4 - ABERDARES NATIONAL PARK:</strong> After breakfast we depart for the Aberdares National Park, enjoying a picnic lunch en route. One of Kenya&rsquo;s most untouched parks, the Aberdares N.P. is a lush forest environment that is home to many species not found on the plains. We arrive in the afternoon and transfer to Treetops Lodge, a fantastic place set around a waterhole which offers superb game viewing opportunities, especially after dark when the animals come to drink. (BLD)<br />
<br />
<strong>Day 5 - LAKE NAKURU:</strong> Leaving the forest behind we head to Lake Nakuru National Park, a Rift Valley lake famed for its enormous concentration of flamingos. The park is also a rhino sanctuary and your chances of spotting both black and white rhino are very good. (BLD)<br />
<br />
<strong>Day 6 - MASAI MARA:</strong> A long drive brings us to the Masai Mara National Reserve. We should have time for an afternoon game drive. (BLD)<br />
<br />

<strong>Days 7-8 - MASAI MARA:</strong> The Masai Mara has one of the highest densities of wildlife to be found anywhere in Africa and during our time here we enjoy a variety of play drives in search of its celebrated residents. We hope to spot lion, leopard, cheetah, buffalo, elephant, giraffe, hippo, hyena and much more. (BLD)<br />
<br />
<strong>Day 9 - NAIROBI:</strong> We leave the Masai Mara behind and head back to Nairobi, arriving in time for lunch. Day use rooms are arranged for the afternoon giving you the chance to relax by the pool or head into town for a final bout of souvenir hunting. Tour ends. (B)<br />
